💀 WELCOME TO THE MURMUR ROOM 💀

October 28, 1923 — Late Night, Manhattan

I couldn’t go straight to the hotel. Something in me demanded movement. Whiskey, maybe. Human voices. Or just the scent of smoke in the night air to remind me I still existed.

I walked the streets of Manhattan until I found a bar that looked out of place — or out of time.

The Murmur Room.

The red neon sign flickered with irregular insistence, casting feverish reflections on the wet sidewalk. It was the first time I saw one of these in person. In 1923, they were still novelty tech — or the sort of thing used by places that knew more than they showed.

The light pulsed as if daring the city to stare back.

I entered.

Inside, muffled music. Scratched jazz. People too quiet for a Saturday. A man with faded eyes read a trembling newspaper. Two women played cards without exchanging a word. The scent was smoke, old varnish, and restrained despair.

I sat at the bar. Ordered bourbon. Double.

Then she arrived.

Wine velvet. Black gloves up to the elbow. Dark hair, stage presence. She walked like she knew how all stories ended — and still wanted one more.

She sat beside me uninvited. Ordered gin. Sipped slowly. Didn’t look at me at first — just at my hand, at the scar between my fingers.

“You’ve held more than just weapons,” she said.
“And you have a voice that never needs to ask twice,” I replied.

She smiled. And I knew it was too late to retreat.

We spoke little.
Going back to the hotel with her felt natural. Like falling. Like sealing a pact.

She didn’t want my name. I didn’t want hers. Just warm skin in the cold night, whispers, tangled sheets.

She was on top of me — riding with ritual intensity. Each movement deliberate, following a rhythm only she could hear.

Her eyes locked on mine. That smile...
Cruel. Delicious. Predatory.

I should’ve pulled away. But I didn’t.

It was a flicker.
Her face changed.

Not like an illusion. More like the truth bleeding through the skin:
Flesh too pale. Eyes too hollow.
Lips darkened. Teeth... wrong. Elongated. Incomplete.
Something watching from beneath the mask.

A living apparition on top of me.

I froze. Heart racing. A scream stuck in my throat.

But then…
It didn’t matter.

The fear was real — for a second. Then it faded like smoke.
Because whatever she was…
Demon.
Predator.
Shadow in human flesh.

She wanted me.
And that was enough.

I gripped her hips. Pulled her closer. I wanted more. I wanted it all.

The creature smiled again — the woman returned — and bent down until her lips brushed mine.

“Now you understand,” she murmured.
“I do,” I answered, not knowing what I meant.

And I let her take me down.
For a few hours, everything was far away. Even the forest. Even the mark on the window.
And that night, I was ready for both.
